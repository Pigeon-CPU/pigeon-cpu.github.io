About

I’m a researcher at the University of Wisconsin–Madison working on antennas and remote sensing, with a focus on UAV-based ground penetrating radar (GPR) and soil volumetric water content sensing. I build end-to-end systems spanning simulation, hardware prototyping, and field experiments.

Research Experience

University of Wisconsin–Madison — Research Assistant (May 2025 – present)

  • Automation: Programmed automated scanning of vector network analyzers using a robotic arm to collect antenna frequency-domain data over 3D radiation patterns.
  • Soil Radar Prototype Design: Designed and integrated a sensor platform for remote sensing of soil volumetric water content.
  • GPR Sim2Real Domain Transfer: Developing algorithms to transfer simulated antenna results to real antenna systems under far-field conditions.

University of Wisconsin–Madison — Undergraduate Research Assistant (Dec 2023 – May 2025)

  • Automation: Built automation for VNA and frequency synthesizer module data acquisition and processing.
  • Antenna Design: Designed/fabricated a wideband dual-polarized cross-shaped Vivaldi antenna (0.2–4.0 GHz).
  • 3D Printing: Designed and printed fixtures for antenna-based experiments.
  • GPR Simulation: Simulated GPR performance with dipole antennas in turf soil conditions.
  • Field Experiment: Conducted field tests with a custom-built compact GPR system; processed data to validate reflection-property correlation with turf water content.

Field Work Highlight

  • Collected field data at the OJ Noer research station using a self-custom-built GPR system; verified feasibility of soil volumetric water content sensing for turf grass fields.
